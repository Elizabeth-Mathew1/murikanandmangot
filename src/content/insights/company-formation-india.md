Forming a company in India involves several decisions at the outset — from choosing the appropriate corporate structure to understanding ongoing compliance obligations.

## Choosing a structure

Private limited companies are common for businesses seeking a separate legal identity and a clear framework for ownership. Partnerships and limited liability partnerships may suit other arrangements, depending on the nature of the venture and the number of promoters involved.

## Registration and documentation

Incorporation requires prescribed forms, memorandum and articles of association, and identity and address documentation for directors and subscribers. Name approval and digital signatures are typically part of the process.

## After incorporation

Once registered, companies must maintain statutory registers, file annual returns, and comply with tax and labour law requirements as applicable. Planning for these obligations early helps avoid administrative difficulties later.

This article provides general information and should not be treated as advice for a particular transaction.
